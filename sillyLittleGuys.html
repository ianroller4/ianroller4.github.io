Silly Little Guys

Design Goals

The goal of the Silly Little Guys was to create a cute creature that players will use to attack enemies, complete puzzles, and complete various tasks that the player themselves cannot do themselves.

Inspiration

The idea for the Silly Little Guys and the game as a whole was heavily inspired by the Nintendo series Pikmin as well as other similar games like Tinykin. and The Wild at Heart. I grew up playing real time strategy games and fell in love with this take on the genre, instead of an overhead view you take control of a character that is on the battlefield. That is with the key difference that you still cannot do anything directyly yourself, you must command your units to complete the various tasks. This creates the feeling of a general on the front line guiding your troops.

With the exception of The Wild at Heart, games of this genre are always 3D. I have always wanted to try adapting the genre to a 2D setting and figure out how the various mechanics that rely on the game being 3D could be translated to the 2D environment.

System Design
  • Players spawn Silly Little Guys by shaking tree
  • Players use Silly Little Guys to collect boat parts, defeat enemies, and complete puzzles by throwing them at the problems
  • Silly Little Guys limit can be increased by defeating enemies and collect special item drop
