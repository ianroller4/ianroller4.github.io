About Me

Hi! My name is Ian Roller.

Who I Am

As long as I can remember, I have loved playing video games. From sitting beside my dad and watching him play Tetris when I was three to the games I play today and every game in between, I have loved and still love video games. I am captivated by the worlds and the characters that fill them. I enjoy the stories, whether happy or sad or something in between. This love has always made me want to try making my own. Even as a child, I would take old CD cases, put a piece of paper with a made-up title and a little drawing into the case, and pretend I had made my own game. I love video games.

Outside of game development I enjoy reading fantasy, sci-fi, and mystery novels, improving my drawing skills, and working on creative writing projects. I also enjoy playing basketball and following the NBA and obviously playing video games.

Design Interests

Gameplay Systems

Developing mechanics that work together to create new experiences.

Game Feel

Fine tuning how the game feels to play to deliver a satisfying experience.

Worldbuilding

The environments, people, cultures, lore that make up the games world.

Exploration Design

Creating a world that is both interesting to explore and rewards players for exploring.

Narrative Design

Designing stories that are captivating and enhance the gameplay experience.

Player Progression

Constructing progression systems to give players a sense of getting stronger without the game feeling easy.

Creative Influences

Gameplay

Growing up I played a lot of strategy games and as I got older the types of games I played expanded to nearly every genre. Through it all, I have always loved games that allowed players to come up with unique solutions to problems and require strategy and thought to win.

I am also a big fan of games with characters that can be descibed as a silly little guy, such as Pikmin and Kirby.

Worldbuilding

As a fan of fantasy and sci-fi I am often inspired by their aesthetics and the themes they present. As expected, I like to build worlds in these genres and push the boundaries of what is expected.

Creative Work

I am often inspired during my creative writing and drawing practice. A character I sketch or a short story I wrote often has become the starting point for a new game idea.
